# Dashboard_of_Performance_Northwind_by_PBI
This is the samples of dashboard of database northwind produced in power BI with query.

Overview
This dashboard provides a full analytical view of the Northwind database, transforming raw transactional data into clear business insights. It highlights how sales evolve over time, which customers and products drive the most revenue, and how performance varies across regions and categories. The goal is to support decision‑making around sales strategy, inventory planning, and customer engagement.

Key Metrics
Total Sales: 1.35M

Yearly Revenue: 1.27M

Total Discount Given: 88.67K

Total Products Sold: 51K

These metrics summarize overall business performance and serve as the foundation for deeper analysis.

Dashboard Sections
1. Revenue by Year
This visualization tracks total revenue across 1996, 1997, and 1998, showing how sales trends evolve over time. It helps identify seasonal patterns, growth periods, and potential slowdowns.

2. Top Customers
A ranked list of customers based on total spending.
Notable high‑value customers include:

QUICK-Stop

Ernst Handel

Save-a-lot Markets

Rattlesnake Canyon Grocery

Hungry Owl All-Night Grocers

This section helps identify key accounts and opportunities for targeted engagement.

3. Revenue by Product Category
Categories such as Dairy Products, Beverages, Confections, and Grains/Cereals are compared by total revenue contribution.
This supports product‑mix optimization and inventory planning.

4. Product-Level Insights
Highlights top‑selling items such as:

Outback Lager

Mascarpone Fabioli

Queso Cabrales

Ipoh Coffee

This section helps identify high‑demand products and potential stock priorities.

5. Geographic Sales Distribution
A world map visualizes revenue by region, including:

Europe

Africa

South America

India

This helps understand market reach and regional performance.

6. Daily Revenue Trends
A time‑series chart showing daily revenue fluctuations from late 1996 through 1998.
Useful for spotting short‑term patterns, promotions impact, and operational bottlenecks.

7. Unit Level vs. Demand
A comparison of product quantities sold versus demand indicators, supporting forecasting and supply chain decisions.
